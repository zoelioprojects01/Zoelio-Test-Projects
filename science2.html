The Science of De-Extinction

Can we really bring extinct animals back to life?

Introduction

De-extinction is the process of reviving extinct species using advanced scientific techniques. It combines genetics, cloning, selective breeding, and biotechnology to bring back creatures that have disappeared from the Earth.

How De-Extinction Works: Step-by-Step

1. DNA Collection

Scientists retrieve ancient DNA from fossils, museum specimens, or frozen tissues.

2. Genome Sequencing

The DNA is sequenced and compared with the closest living relatives to identify missing or damaged parts.

3. Genetic Engineering

Using tools like CRISPR, scientists edit the genome of a living species to match the extinct one.

4. Cloning or Embryo Implantation

The engineered DNA is inserted into an egg cell and implanted in a surrogate mother for development.

5. Birth and Adaptation

If successful, the animal is born and must be raised in a habitat suitable for its survival.
