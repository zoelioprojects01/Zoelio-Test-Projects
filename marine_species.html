Endangered Marine Species

Vaquita

Scientific Name: Phocoena sinus

Habitat: Shallow coastal waters

Current Population: Less than 10 individuals

Subregion: Northern Gulf of California

Cause of Endangerment: Bycatch in illegal fishing nets (gillnets)

Hawksbill Sea Turtle

Scientific Name: Eretmochelys imbricata

Habitat: Coral reefs

Current Population: ~15,000 nesting females

Subregion: Tropical Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans

Cause of Endangerment: Illegal shell trade, habitat loss

North Atlantic Right Whale

Scientific Name: Eubalaena glacialis

Habitat: Coastal waters of the North Atlantic

Current Population: ~340 individuals

Subregion: Eastern seaboard of North America

Cause of Endangerment: Ship strikes, entanglement in fishing gear

Blue Whale

Scientific Name: Balaenoptera musculus

Habitat: Open oceans worldwide

Current Population: ~10,000–25,000

Subregion: Global

Cause of Endangerment: Ship collisions, past commercial whaling

Giant Manta Ray

Scientific Name: Mobula birostris

Habitat: Tropical, subtropical oceans

Current Population: Vulnerable, population declining

Subregion: Indo-Pacific and Atlantic

Cause of Endangerment: Overfishing, gillnets, climate change

Chinese Paddlefish

Scientific Name: Psephurus gladius

Habitat: Yangtze River

Current Population: Functionally extinct (0)

Subregion: China

Cause of Endangerment: Dam construction, overfishing

Dugong

Scientific Name: Dugong dugon

Habitat: Seagrass beds in coastal waters

Current Population: Around 100,000 globally, critically endangered in East Africa

Subregion: Indian Ocean, western Pacific

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat destruction, hunting

Scalloped Hammerhead Shark

Scientific Name: Sphyrna lewini

Habitat: Coastal warm waters

Current Population: Critically endangered, population declining

Subregion: Worldwide tropics

Cause of Endangerment: Shark finning, overfishing

Leatherback Sea Turtle

Scientific Name: Dermochelys coriacea

Habitat: Open oceans and tropical beaches

Current Population: ~34,000 nesting females

Subregion: Pacific, Atlantic, Indian Oceans

Cause of Endangerment: Fishing gear, plastic ingestion

European Eel

Scientific Name: Anguilla anguilla

Habitat: Freshwater and marine

Current Population: Critically endangered, population declining

Subregion: European rivers to Sargasso Sea

Cause of Endangerment: Dams, overfishing, pollution

Banggai Cardinalfish

Scientific Name: Pterapogon kauderni

Habitat: Coral reefs

Current Population: Fewer than 2 million, declining

Subregion: Indonesia

Cause of Endangerment: Aquarium trade, habitat destruction

Galápagos Sea Lion

Scientific Name: Zalophus wollebaeki

Habitat: Rocky beaches and coastal areas

Current Population: ~20,000–50,000

Subregion: Galápagos Islands

Cause of Endangerment: Disease, climate change, fishing

Coelacanth

Scientific Name: Latimeria chalumnae

Habitat: Deep sea

Current Population: A few hundred known

Subregion: Indian Ocean

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat disturbance, accidental capture

Southern Bluefin Tuna

Scientific Name: Thunnus maccoyii

Habitat: Open ocean

Current Population: <13% of historical levels

Subregion: Southern Hemisphere oceans

Cause of Endangerment: Overfishing

Smalltooth Sawfish

Scientific Name: Pristis pectinata

Habitat: Shallow coastal and estuarine waters

Current Population: Fewer than 1,000 individuals

Subregion: Western Atlantic, Gulf of Mexico

Cause of Endangerment: Bycatch, habitat degradation
