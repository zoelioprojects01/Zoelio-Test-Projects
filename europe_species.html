Endangered Species of Europe

European Bison

Scientific Name: Bison bonasus

Habitat: Forests and woodlands

Current Population: Over 6,000 individuals

Subregion: Eastern Europe (Poland, Belarus, Russia)

Cause of Endangerment: Overhunting, habitat loss, and disease

Iberian Lynx

Scientific Name: Lynx pardinus

Habitat: Scrublands, forests, and rocky hills

Current Population: Around 400 individuals

Subregion: Iberian Peninsula (Spain and Portugal)

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, prey depletion, and hunting

European Wildcat

Scientific Name: Felis silvestris

Habitat: Forests, grasslands, and scrublands

Current Population: Around 10,000 individuals

Subregion: Across Europe

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, hybridization with domestic cats, and persecution

Mediterranean Monk Seal

Scientific Name: Monachus monachus

Habitat: Coastal caves and rocky shores

Current Population: Estimated at around 700 individuals

Subregion: Mediterranean Sea

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, pollution, and hunting

European Sturgeon

Scientific Name: Acipenser sturio

Habitat: Coastal waters and large rivers

Current Population: Fewer than 1,000 individuals

Subregion: Western Europe (e.g., Gironde Estuary, France)

Cause of Endangerment: Overfishing, habitat degradation, and pollution

Alpine Ibex

Scientific Name: Capra ibex

Habitat: Rocky mountain slopes and high-altitude regions

Current Population: Over 3,000 individuals

Subregion: Alps (Switzerland, France, Italy)

Cause of Endangerment: Overhunting and habitat loss

Bearded Vulture

Scientific Name: Gypaetus barbatus

Habitat: High mountains and rocky cliffs

Current Population: About 500 individuals in Europe

Subregion: Pyrenees, Alps, and Mediterranean mountain ranges

Cause of Endangerment: Poisoning, habitat disturbance, and human persecution

Golden Eagle

Scientific Name: Aquila chrysaetos

Habitat: Rocky mountains, forests, and open plains

Current Population: Around 5,000 pairs in Europe

Subregion: Across Europe

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, hunting, and poisoning

European Turtle Dove

Scientific Name: Streptopelia turtur

Habitat: Open woodlands, farmland, and scrublands

Current Population: Fewer than 200,000 individuals

Subregion: Southern Europe

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, hunting, and climate change

Scotch Argus Butterfly

Scientific Name: Erebia aethiops

Habitat: Grassy meadows and heathlands

Current Population: Vulnerable, population declining

Subregion: United Kingdom and parts of Europe

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, agricultural intensification, and climate change

Corsican Nuthatch

Scientific Name: Sitta whiteheadi

Habitat: Pine forests

Current Population: About 1,500 individuals

Subregion: Corsica, France

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss and fragmentation

Cantabrian Chamois

Scientific Name: Rupicapra pyrenaica parva

Habitat: Mountainous regions and rocky slopes

Current Population: Around 15,000 individuals

Subregion: Cantabrian Mountains, Spain

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, overhunting, and competition with livestock

European Eel

Scientific Name: Anguilla anguilla

Habitat: Freshwater rivers, lakes, and coastal areas

Current Population: Critically endangered, population declining

Subregion: Europe, migrating to the Sargasso Sea

Cause of Endangerment: Overfishing, habitat degradation, and barriers to migration

Grey Wolves

Scientific Name: Canis lupus

Habitat: Forests, tundra, and grasslands

Current Population: Around 12,000 individuals

Subregion: Eastern Europe, Russia

Cause of Endangerment: Hunting, habitat fragmentation, and conflict with humans

Marbled White Butterfly

Scientific Name: Melanargia galathea

Habitat: Grasslands and meadows

Current Population: Vulnerable, population declining

Subregion: Central and Southern Europe

Cause of Endangerment: Agricultural intensification, habitat loss, and climate change
