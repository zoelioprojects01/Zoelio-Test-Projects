Endangered Species of Africa

Mountain Gorilla

Scientific Name: Gorilla beringei beringei

Habitat: Forests of Uganda, Rwanda, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC).

Current Population: Approximately 1,000 individuals

Subregion: Central Africa

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat destruction, poaching, human-wildlife conflict, disease transmission from humans (e.g., respiratory infections).

African Wild Dog

Scientific Name: Lycaon pictus

Habitat: Savannas, arid zones, and woodlands across sub-Saharan Africa.

Current Population: Around 6,000 individuals.

Subregion: Southern and Eastern Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, diseases such as rabies, and poaching.

African Elephant

Scientific Name: Loxodonta africana

Habitat: Savannas, forests, and desert areas of Africa.

Current Population: Approximately 415,000 individuals.

Subregion: Sub-Saharan Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Poaching for ivory, habitat loss, and human-wildlife conflict.

Cheetah

Scientific Name: Acinonyx jubatus

Habitat: Grasslands and open savannas of Africa.

Current Population: Estimated 7,100 individuals.

Subregion: Eastern and Southern Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, human-wildlife conflict, and loss of prey.

Black Rhino

Scientific Name: Diceros bicornis

Habitat: Scrub and savanna in Eastern and Southern Africa.

Current Population: Approximately 5,000 individuals.

Subregion: Southern and Eastern Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Poaching for horns, habitat loss, and human-wildlife conflict.

Congo Gorilla

Scientific Name: Gorilla gorilla gorilla

Habitat: Rainforests in the Congo Basin of Central Africa.

Current Population: Around 100,000 individuals.

Subregion: Central Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, poaching, disease transmission, and human-wildlife conflict.

African Penguin

Scientific Name: Spheniscus demersus

Habitat: Coastal regions of South Africa and Namibia.

Current Population: Approximately 50,000 individuals.

Subregion: Southern Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat destruction, oil spills, overfishing, and climate change.

White Rhinoceros

Scientific Name: Ceratotherium simum

Habitat: Grasslands and savannas of Southern Africa.

Current Population: Approximately 18,000 individuals.

Subregion: Southern Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Poaching for horns, loss of habitat, and human-wildlife conflict.

Eastern Lowland Gorilla

Scientific Name: Gorilla beringei graueri

Habitat: Rainforests in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC).

Current Population: Estimated 3,800 individuals.

Subregion: Central Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, poaching, disease transmission, and civil conflict.

Pygmy Hippopotamus

Scientific Name: Choeropsis liberiensis

Habitat: Forested swamps and rivers of West Africa.

Current Population: Estimated 2,000 individuals.

Subregion: Western Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat destruction, hunting, and human encroachment.

Saiga Antelope

Scientific Name: Saiga tatarica

Habitat: Steppes of Central Asia, with some populations in Africa.

Current Population: Approximately 100,000 individuals.

Subregion: Central Asia (mainly) and Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Poaching, habitat loss, and disease.

Spiny-Tailed Lizard

Scientific Name: Uromastyx spp.

Habitat: Deserts and rocky areas of Northern Africa.

Current Population: Vulnerable to extinction.

Subregion: Northern Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat destruction, illegal pet trade, and over-hunting.

Rothschild Giraffe

Scientific Name: Giraffa camelopardalis rothschildi

Habitat: Grasslands and woodlands of Uganda and Kenya.

Current Population: Approximately 1,100 individuals.

Subregion: Eastern Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss and poaching.

Okapi

Scientific Name: Okapia johnstoni

Habitat: Dense rainforests of the Democratic Republic of Congo.

Current Population: Estimated 10,000 individuals.

Subregion: Central Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss and poaching.

Dugong

Scientific Name: Dugong dugon

Habitat: Coastal areas of East Africa and Red Sea.

Current Population: Estimated 100,000 individuals.

Subregion: Eastern Africa.

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat destruction, hunting, and boat collisions.
