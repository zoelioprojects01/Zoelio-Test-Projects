Endangered Species of South America

Jaguar

Scientific Name: Panthera onca

Habitat: Rainforests, grasslands, and scrublands

Current Population: Fewer than 20,000 individuals

Subregion: South and Central America

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict

Giant River Otter

Scientific Name: Pteronura brasiliensis

Habitat: Amazon and Orinoco River basins

Current Population: Approximately 5,000 individuals

Subregion: Amazon Basin, Brazil

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat destruction, pollution, and hunting

Andean Condor

Scientific Name: Vultur gryphus

Habitat: Mountainous regions, particularly the Andes

Current Population: Around 6,700 individuals

Subregion: Andes Mountains, South America

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, hunting, and poisoning

Spix’s Macaw (Little Blue Macaw)

Scientific Name: Cyanopsitta spixii

Habitat: Brazilian rainforests and woodlands

Current Population: Extinct in the wild, around 200 individuals in captivity

Subregion: Brazil

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, poaching, and illegal trade

Brazilian Tapir

Scientific Name: Tapirus terrestris

Habitat: Rainforests, wetlands, and lowland forests

Current Population: Estimated to be around 10,000 individuals

Subregion: Brazil, Bolivia, Ecuador, and other Amazon regions

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss and hunting

South American Sea Lion

Scientific Name: Otaria flavescens

Habitat: Coastal regions and islands

Current Population: Over 200,000 individuals

Subregion: Southern South America (Argentina, Chile, Uruguay)

Cause of Endangerment: Hunting, habitat disturbance, and fishing net entanglements

Golden Lion Tamarin

Scientific Name: Leontopithecus rosalia

Habitat: Tropical rainforests of Brazil

Current Population: About 3,200 individuals

Subregion: Brazil

Cause of Endangerment: Deforestation, illegal pet trade, and habitat fragmentation

Hyacinth Macaw

Scientific Name: Anodorhynchus hyacinthinus

Habitat: Savannas, woodlands, and palm forests

Current Population: Around 6,500 individuals

Subregion: Brazil, Bolivia, Paraguay

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss, illegal pet trade, and poaching

Chacoan Peccary

Scientific Name: Catagonus wagneri

Habitat: Dry forests and scrublands

Current Population: Around 1,000 individuals

Subregion: Chaco region, Argentina, Paraguay, Bolivia

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss and hunting

Pygmy Marmoset

Scientific Name: Cebuella pygmaea

Habitat: Tropical rainforests, especially lowland areas

Current Population: Unknown, but declining

Subregion: Amazon Basin, Colombia, Brazil, Ecuador, Peru

Cause of Endangerment: Deforestation and illegal pet trade

Colombian Spider Monkey

Scientific Name: Ateles fusciceps rufiventris

Habitat: Lowland tropical forests

Current Population: Less than 1,000 individuals

Subregion: Colombia

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat destruction, hunting, and illegal pet trade

Andean Bear

Scientific Name: Tremarctos ornatus

Habitat: Mountainous cloud forests and paramo

Current Population: Estimated around 2,000 individuals

Subregion: Andes Mountains, South America

Cause of Endangerment: Habitat loss and hunting

White-cheeked Pintail

Scientific Name: Anas bahamensis

Habitat: Wetlands and coastal areas

Current Population: Vulnerable, population unknown

Subregion: Coastal areas of South America (Argentina, Chile, Peru)

Cause of Endangerment: Wetland destruction and climate change

Peruvian Frog (Titicaca Water Frog)

Scientific Name: Telmatobius culeus

Habitat: Mountainous lakes and streams

Current Population: Fewer than 1,000 individuals

Subregion: Peru

Cause of Endangerment: Pollution, disease (chytridiomycosis), and habitat destruction

Galapagos Penguin

Scientific Name: Spheniscus mendiculus

Habitat: Coastal areas and islands

Current Population: Around 1,200 individuals

Subregion: Galapagos Islands, Ecuador

Cause of Endangerment: Climate change, overfishing, and oil pollution
